Peaches

Colors

  • Base coat
  • Sheer white
  • Light golden orange
  • Mustard yellow
  • Dark red
  • Burnt orange

Tools

  • 1 small brush for light orange and yellow
  • 1 small brush for dark red and burnt orange

Instructions

I. Start by applying base coat and a sheer white color. Depending on your preference, use 2-3 coats of the sheer white to give a clean base for the design. Streaks in the white base will be covered by the design painted over it.

II. Using one of your small brushes, paint a small crescent/wedge-like shape on each nail with the golden orange color. These will act as the foundation of the peach design and will look near the edges of your nails or towards the cuticle. Don't wait for this color to fully dry before moving on to the next step!

III. Still using the same brush from step 2, paint small highlights with the mustard yellow. If the base orange is still slightly wet, this will naturally blend the colors to create more depth. Some streaks and uneven texture is normal and will be smoothed out later by top coat.

IV. Switch your brush and use the dark red color to line the bases of the peaches you painted. Since this is the peel of the peach, it helps to have the middle of the curve be thicker to create a sense of dimension!

V. Continue to use the same brush and dark red color to paint the centers of the peach slices. Less polish on the brush will give this part more texture since the color will naturally vary in darkness. Using a light stippling/dabbing motion with the brush will also help give texture.

VI. As an optional step, you can add some burnt orange to the peels of the peach slices for more color variation. This should be rather subtle (best seen on the pointer finger), and best results would be achieved with the first brush that was used for the orange and yellow. The old polish on the brush will further blend more color into the red of the peel.
